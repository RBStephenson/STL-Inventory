Assembly-Line Sequence

Complete each stage across ALL models before moving to the next. Never finish one model completely before touching the others.

1
Prep & Prime

[PLACEHOLDER: Clean mold lines, base prime color, prime all models the same. E.g. "P-005 Dark Neutral Gray via airbrush, 30+ PSI, 0.5mm. Full coverage. Allow 30 min dry time."]

2
Main Body Base Coat

[PLACEHOLDER: Largest color area, all models. E.g. "Speedpaint 2.0 [color] over entire armour — one pass, no reworking while wet." Or airbrush base coat recipe.]

3
Shade Pass

[PLACEHOLDER: Wash all models. E.g. "Strong Tone or Nuln Oil into all recesses. Let dry fully — minimum 30 min — before touching."]

4
Secondary Colours

[PLACEHOLDER: Paint each secondary color in turn across ALL models. E.g. "Pauldron bone trim → all 10 models. Then parchment scrolls → all 10. Then leather pouches → all 10."]

5
Highlight Pass

[PLACEHOLDER: Drybrush or edge highlight, all models same pass. E.g. "Light drybrush of [highlight color] over all armour edges — all models at once."]

6
Details Round

[PLACEHOLDER: Eyes, gems, faction icons, scrollwork — one detail type at a time across all. E.g. "Eyes → all 10. Then lenses → all 10. Then chapter markings → all 10."]

7
Basing

All models together. Never base one model while others are mid-paint — the base must be consistent across the unit. See Basing tab for full recipe.

8
Varnish

All models at once. Matt varnish overall → spot gloss on gems, eyes, liquids → satin on leather/skin if applicable.

Quality Tiers

Three tracks — same base recipe, different stopping points. Choose based on your time budget and the role of this unit in your army.

Tier 1

Tabletop Ready

[PLACEHOLDER: ~20–40 min/model at pace]
  • Prime + base colour + wash + drybrush highlight
  • Key details only: eyes (if very visible), faction icon, main weapon accent
  • Basing complete
  • Matte varnish
  • [PLACEHOLDER: Speedpaint 2.0 fits perfectly here — one-pass basecoat + shade]
  • Stop point: After the drybrush highlight — looks solid at arm's length.
Tier 2

Battle Ready

[PLACEHOLDER: ~45–90 min/model]
  • Everything in Tier 1, plus:
  • Edge highlights on primary surfaces (armour panels, blade edges)
  • Second wash pass in deepest recesses for sharper contrast
  • Faces and eyes painted carefully (if visible at scale)
  • Small distinguishing details: purity seals, scrollwork, trophies
  • Tidy lining on major colour separations
  • [PLACEHOLDER: any tier-specific step for this unit]
  • Stop point: After edge highlights — looks intentional and finished up close.
Tier 3

Display Quality

[PLACEHOLDER: ~2–8+ hours/model]
  • Everything in Tier 2, plus:
  • Smooth blending on key surfaces (wetblend, feathering, or glazing)
  • OSL if a light-emitting source is present
  • NMM for metallic surfaces (ask before committing)
  • Multiple glaze passes for colour richness and depth
  • Object source lighting consistency across the entire model
  • High-polish base with environmental storytelling
  • Spot gloss on gems, lenses, wet effects
  • [PLACEHOLDER: any display-specific technique for this unit]
  • Stop point: When you're happy with it. There is no ceiling.
✦ ARMY ADVICE: For large units (10+), paint to Tier 1 and add Tier 2 finishing to any character models or unit champions. Your time is better spent getting an entire army to Tier 1 than having 3 perfect models and 20 unfinished ones.

Brush Skills

Techniques used in this guide — practical notes for the workbench.

Edge Highlighting
Technique

Edge Highlighting

[PLACEHOLDER: thin your highlight paint to 1:2. Load a fine brush (size 1 or 0). Touch the tip to a sharp armour edge and draw along it steadily. The paint should flow off the brush with minimal pressure. Aim for a consistent 1-pixel-wide line. Practice on a spare model first — steadiness comes with repetition, not rushing.]

Drybrushing
Technique

Drybrushing

[PLACEHOLDER: load your brush with undiluted paint, then wipe on tissue paper until almost no colour transfers. Brush lightly over raised surfaces with quick, multi-directional strokes. The paint deposits only on peaks — recesses stay dark. The key is patience: multiple light passes are better than one heavy stroke. A stiff, splayed brush (old rounds work great) gives more control than a flat.]

Wet Palette for Batches
Technique

Wet Palette — Batch Consistency

[PLACEHOLDER: mix enough of each colour to cover the entire batch in one go — err on the side of too much. Place mixed colours on the wet palette to keep them workable for the full session. Between models, remix if the consistency shifts (palette will thin slightly over time). The same mix on every model is the foundation of army cohesion.]
