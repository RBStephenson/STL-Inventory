Iron Hands — Shattered Legions — survivors of the Isstvan V Dropsite Massacre. Cold black armor with a deliberate blue-grey undertone distinguishes them from Raven Guard's warmer black. The bionics are the defining visual — every Iron Hand replaces flesh with steel, and at 28mm the contrast between the matte black armor and the bright VMC metallics is what sells the scheme. Weathering is not optional on a Shattered Legions force: chipped, battle-damaged, grimy — these warriors have been fighting a guerrilla war for months. Light source zenithal. Priority: armor tone first, bionics second, battle damage third.

Step 2 · Airbrush

White Zenithal

P-003 White Primer undiluted, 0.5mm, 30+ PSI, strictly from above. Top surfaces get white, sides get partial, undersides stay black. At 28mm this subtle value gradient is the foundation that makes the armor read as three-dimensional rather than a flat black shape.

Weathering

Shattered Legions marines are survivors — their armor has been battle-damaged, field-repaired, and worn for months of guerrilla warfare since Isstvan V. Heavy weathering is not optional; it's what makes a Shattered Legions force read as distinct from a pristine parade-ground Space Marine army. Apply these steps after all base painting and before varnishing.

⚠ ORDER OF OPERATIONS: Complete all armor, bionic, and detail steps first. Weather last. Weathering over incomplete base colors looks muddy. Varnish after weathering — it seals everything and protects the chips.
Battle Damage — Sponge Chipping
Step 1 · Brush / Sponge

Paint Chip — Exposed Metal

Tear a small piece of blister foam or kitchen sponge to a jagged edge. Dab Cool Grey AMP-002 onto the sponge, dab off excess on palette, then lightly dab the sponge against armor surfaces. The irregular foam texture creates random chip patterns that read as paint worn off by combat impacts, revealing bare metal beneath. Focus on high-wear areas: knee pads, shoulder pad rims, helmet edges, forearm plates. Leave the chest relatively clean — it faces forward and takes the most attention.

Cool Grey AMP-002

VMC Handling Notes
Technique · VMC Airbrush

Vallejo Metal Color in the Airbrush

VMC is pre-thinned for 0.4mm nozzle at 1:1 with distilled water. For 0.3mm, thin to 1:1.5. Always agitate the cup every 2–3 minutes — metallic pigment settles fast enough to cause grainy output before you finish a squad. No Flow Improver needed; VMC already has low surface tension. Apply over gloss or semi-gloss black for maximum reflectivity on bionic surfaces.

✦ TIP: Test VMC on a spare sprue before each session. Metallic paints behave differently at different temperatures — a mix that worked yesterday may need slight adjustment today.
Troubleshooting

Blowback / Bubbling

Cause: Too much air, trigger pressed fully without pulling back, or overfull cup. Fix: Reduce PSI by 5, pull trigger back slowly while air is flowing first. Never fill the cup more than 2/3. With VMC specifically, a very full cup can slosh and cause blowback at high PSI.

Grainy VMC Metallic

Cause: Metallic pigment settled in cup during painting session, or paint too thick for the nozzle. Fix: Agitate the cup with a toothpick between passes. If the output is still grainy, the pigment has settled too far — empty the cup, agitate the bottle vigorously for 60 seconds, refill and re-test. Over-thinning also causes graininess — stay at 1:1 for 0.3mm.

Uneven Coverage / Banding

Cause: Inconsistent trigger pressure or stopping mid-stroke. Fix: Constant speed and pressure across each stroke. Start and end off the model. Practice the motion on scrap before applying to figures. For zenithal metallic passes, sweep in broad arcs rather than short dabbing motions.

Brush Skills

Edge highlighting on black armor, sponge chipping for battle damage, targeted washing for metallics, and VMC brush application technique.

Technique · Edge Highlighting — Black Armor

Confident Edge Strokes on Dark Surfaces

Black armor edge highlighting is unforgiving because every wavering line is visible against the dark field. Load a size 0 round with thinned paint (1:1.5 for Slate Grey AMP-013), wipe to near-dry on palette, and drag the tip along the edge in one committed stroke from corner to corner. Do not pause mid-stroke. Rest your little finger on the model for stability. If the line wobbles, let it dry and carefully correct with a thin Coal Black 002 line on the offending edge, then re-highlight. Two thin lines stacked are better than one uneven thick one.

✦ TIP: Work the first edge pass across all models in the unit before starting the second pass. The consistency of a squad in rotation is better than perfecting individual models one at a time.
Technique · Sponge Chipping

Battle Damage — Paint Chip Technique

Tear (don't cut) a small piece of blister foam or kitchen sponge to get a jagged, irregular edge. Load with thinned metallic or grey paint, then dab excess off on a paper towel until the sponge barely deposits color. Touch lightly to armor surfaces in the wear areas — knee, shoulder, forearm. The irregular foam texture creates naturally random chip patterns. Build up slowly in multiple light dabs — you can always add more; removing chips means repainting. For a Shattered Legions force, the chips should be noticeably heavier than a standard marines army.

Technique · Metallic Wash Control

Targeted Wash on Metallic Surfaces

Metallic surfaces require targeted washing rather than flooding. Thin Black Wash 200 at 1:1. Load a fine round brush, touch the tip to a recess or joint, and let capillary action pull the wash in. Do not drag the brush across metallic surfaces — it leaves tide marks that flatten the shine. If the wash pools on a flat metallic surface, pick it up immediately with a dry brush. The goal is shadow in joints and seams, not a uniform wash across the whole bionic.

⚠ NOTE: Apply targeted washes before final specular highlights. The wash darkens recesses; the highlight brightens peaks. Both are needed for metallic depth at 28mm scale.
Technique · Drybrushing for Ash Dust

Ash Accumulation on Lower Armor

Load a stiff flat brush (a dedicated drybrush or old worn brush) with Bright Neutral Grey 045. Wipe on cloth until barely any paint deposits. Skim the brush across the bottom half of leg armor and boot tops in light horizontal strokes — mimicking ash settled by gravity. The goal is a very faint grey dusting that reads as accumulated volcanic ash rather than a heavy coat. Feather it out so it's heaviest at the very bottom and disappears by mid-shin.
