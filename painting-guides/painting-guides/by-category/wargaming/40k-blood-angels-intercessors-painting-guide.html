Blood Angels Intercessors — The sons of Sanguinius wear their red as a mark of both nobility and barely-contained fury. Deep blood-red power armour sits against black weapons and gold trim in a classic warm/cool contrast — the gold lifts the palette into something regal; the black grounds it. Light source is zenithal (top-down), pushing strong contrast so the red reads clearly at arm's length across a gaming table. The Baal desert base — warm ochre cracked earth — reinforces the warm palette and gives the whole army an arid, battle-worn feel.

60/30/10 Rule: 60% blood-red armour · 30% black weapons and backpack · 10% gold trim and purity seals.

Batch Painting Workflow

5 Intercessors · Brush-focused · Target time: 30 min/model (Tier 1) · 75 min/model (Tier 2)

✦ ARMY COHESION: Build and undercoat all five bases before touching any paint on the models. Your Baal desert recipe locks in now — every future Blood Angels model must match.
Assembly-Line Sequence

Complete each stage across all 5 models before moving on. The temptation to finish one fully before the others is real — resist it.

1
Prep & Prime — All 5 Models

Clean any mold lines (Intercessors have some on the pauldrons — worth catching now). Apply basing texture to all bases. Prime all models: P-005 Dark Neutral Gray for the Speedpaint route (Tier 1), or P-002 Black for the layered route (Tier 2+). 30+ PSI, 0.5mm nozzle if airbrushing. Allow 30 min minimum before painting.

2
Main Body — Red Armour, All 5

Tier 1: Apply Blood Red Speedpaint 2.0 to all red armour panels — one pass, no reworking while wet. Let dry fully (30 min). Tier 2+: Burgundy 069 base coat, then Bold Pyrrole Red 003 over all armour — both passes across all 5 before moving on.

3
Shade Pass — All 5

Wash red armour recesses and any gold areas on all 5 models. Let dry completely — minimum 30 min. Do not rush this; wet wash = smeared highlights.

4
Secondary Colours — One Type at a Time, All 5

Paint each colour in turn across the whole batch: black backpacks → all 5 · black bolter casings → all 5 · gold trim → all 5 · purity seal parchment → all 5. Do not switch models mid-colour.

5
Highlight Pass — All 5

Drybrush red armour edges on all 5 (Tier 1 stopping point). Then edge highlight red panels on all 5 (Tier 2+). One highlight type across all models before moving to the next.

6
Details Round — One Detail Type, All 5

Eyes → all 5 · Blood drop icons → all 5 · purity seal script → all 5 · helmet trim if any → all 5. Wet palette is essential here — load enough for the full batch.

7
Basing — All 5 Together

Complete all basing steps across all 5 models simultaneously. See Basing tab. Never base one model while others are mid-paint.

8
Varnish — All 5 at Once

Matt varnish over entire model. Then targeted brush-on gloss on lenses, eyes, any blood effects. Satin on bare skin if present.

Wet Palette Protocol
✦ WET PALETTE: For a batch of 5, all your mixes go on the wet palette — especially the red highlight mix. Load enough for the full batch before starting any model. The same pool on all five models is what gives an army its cohesion.

Tier 1 — Tabletop Ready (Speedpaint Route)
Step 2T1 · Brush

Blood Red — One-Pass Speedpaint

Over the grey prime, apply Blood Red Speedpaint 2.0 to all red armour surfaces. Flow it on with a loaded brush — let it settle into recesses naturally. One pass: guide it, then leave it. Overworking while wet activates the self-leveling agent and ruins the blend. This single step gives you base colour + shade + depth simultaneously.

Blood Red
Speedpaint 2.0
~45% value — one-pass armour
✦ TIP: Apply to one panel area at a time — large areas left open too long before you touch them can dry-streak. Work panel by panel down the model.
Step 3T1 · Drybrush

Broad Edge Highlight — Tier 1 Stopping Point

Once Blood Red is fully dry (minimum 30 min), lightly drybrush all raised edges and panel corners. Load brush, wipe until barely any colour transfers, then quick multi-directional strokes across panel edges, pauldron rims, and helmet ridges. This reads as highlighted armour at arm's length on the tabletop.

~62% value — drybrush highlight
✦ TIER 1 STOP: After this step, your armour is done for Tabletop Ready. Complete details and basing, then varnish. It reads solidly at arm's length. Proceed below only for Tier 2+.
Tier 2+ — Layered Red (Over Black Prime)
Step 2T2 · Brush

Deep Shadow Base — Burgundy

Over black prime, apply Burgundy 069 thinned to 1:2 across all armour surfaces. The black prime will show through in the deepest recesses — that's correct. This establishes the warm shadow tone across all panels. 2–3 thin passes to build solid coverage without obscuring the recess shadows. Do not cover the black completely.

Burgundy 069
Pro Acryl
~22% value — warm shadow base
✦ TIP: This step should read as a very dark red at arm's length — darker than finished armour, and that's right. The value will climb with each subsequent step.
Step 3T2 · Brush

Mid-Red Layer

Bold Pyrrole Red 003 thinned 1:2. Apply to the upper two-thirds of each armour panel, leaving Burgundy visible in the lower thirds and recesses. Build in 2–3 passes rather than flooding it on — this preserves the value gradient. Apply to all raised planes: pauldron faces, chest plate faces, helmet crown, knee caps.

Bold Pyrrole Red 003
Pro Acryl
~48% value — main armour colour
Step 4T2 · Wash

Recess Shade

Thin AP Fanatic Red Tone (or Dark Red Tone for colder recesses) to 1:1 with water. Carefully apply into recesses, panel seams, and armour joints using a small brush — don't flood the surfaces. The wash should settle into the crevices, not pool on the faces. Let dry 30 min completely.

✦ TIP: If Red Tone pools unnaturally on a flat surface, wick it back immediately with a barely-damp brush tip. Once dry it's much harder to fix.
Step 5T2 · Brush

Highlight Layer — Red Orange

AMP 017 Red Orange thinned 1:2. Apply to the upper third of each panel face — the area that would catch the most zenithal light. Pauldron upper surface, chest plate upper area, helmet crown. Keep it away from recesses. This shifts the value up and adds the warm orange-red that gives Blood Angels their characteristic luminous quality.

Step 6T2 · Brush

Edge Highlight — Tier 2 Stopping Point

Orange Red S31 thinned 1:2, fine brush. Drag along the sharpest armour edges — pauldron rims, chest plate corners, knee cap ridge, helmet brow ridge, boot toe. Keep the line 1–2 pixels wide. This is the step that makes Battle Ready look intentional. At arm's length it reads as crisp, deliberate, well-executed.

Orange Red S31
Pro Acryl

✦ TIER 2 STOP: After edge highlights, your armour is Battle Ready. Continue below for Display quality only.
Step 7T3 · Filter — Tier 3 Only

Glaze for Depth & Richness

Thin Burgundy 069 heavily (1:5 with water) and glaze back into the lower third of each panel to deepen the shadow gradient. This pushes the value range wider and gives the armour a richer, more three-dimensional quality. Apply 2–3 very thin passes — each pass should be barely visible individually. Build cumulative depth.

Burgundy 069
Pro Acryl
~22% value — shadow glaze
⚠ NOTE: If you want to add a unifying warm glaze — a touch of Transparent Red 047 (1:8 with water) over the mid-red planes — use FW Crimson Ink instead if thinning significantly. Transparent Red 047 shifts magenta when heavily thinned and will push the armour purple.

Basing — Baal Desert Ruins

Cracked ochre earth with sparse dead grass and rubble fragments — the arid surface of Baal Secundus. Warm sandy tones reinforce the army's hot palette.

✦ ARMY RULE: Build all five bases before painting any armour. This recipe must match every Blood Angels model you paint from this point forward — establish it now and keep a note of the colours used.
Base Theme: Cracked Desert Earth
Step 1 · Finish

Apply Sand & Grit Texture

Coat the base with undiluted PVA glue. Press in a mix of fine hobby sand and a few small grit pieces (3–4 per base) for natural variation. Let cure fully — at least 2 hours, ideally overnight. No bare plastic showing.

✦ TIP: A mix of fine sand (dominant) and medium grit (sparse) gives better visual texture than either alone. Push a few larger pieces against the model's feet for anchor points.
Step 2 · Airbrush / Brush

Prime Black Over Texture

P-002 Black over all base texture and the base rim. This seals the sand (prevents flaking) and creates the shadow baseline for the basing colour. Allow 20 min dry time.

P-002 Black
Pro Acryl Primer
~3% value — prime and seal
Step 3 · Brush

Base Ground Colour — Ochre Earth

Khaki 061 thinned 1:2, stippled over all base texture. Leave black in the deepest crevices between sand grains — that's your shadow depth. Two passes for solid coverage on raised areas. The warm tan is the foundation of the Baal look.

Khaki 061
Pro Acryl
~50% value — desert ground
Step 4 · Wash

Shade — Warm Ochre Wash

Brown Wash 201 thinned 1:1 with water, washed across the entire base surface. Flows into sand crevices, deepens shadows between grit pieces, unifies the texture. Let dry 20 min.

Brown Wash 201
Pro Acryl

Bone S23, barely loaded brush, light strokes over all raised texture peaks — sand grain tips, grit surfaces, rubble edges. This is what makes the basing look sun-bleached and arid. Build slowly with 2 passes — don't overdo it into uniformity.

Second Drybrush — Pale Highlight

Bright Ivory 022, very lightly loaded, over only the very topmost texture peaks. Optional but pushes the sense of strong sunlight beating down — very effective against the dark blood-red armour.

Bright Ivory 022

Step 6 · Finish

Detail Elements — Rubble & Tufts

A few sparse tufts of dead/dry grass (warm brown-ochre coloured, not green — Baal is arid, not temperate). 1–2 tufts per base, clustered near grit pieces. Optional: a small stone or broken masonry piece painted Dark Neutral Grey 044 → drybrush Bright Neutral Grey 045, suggests ruined structures.

✦ TIP: Resist the urge to cover the base in tufts. Sparse is more realistic for desert. The sand texture is the feature — tufts are accents only.

Base Rim — Black

Coal Black 002, undiluted, around the entire base rim. Every model in the army — same coal black rim. No exceptions. This is the signature that reads as a unified force on the table.

Quality Tiers

Three tracks — same foundation recipe, different stopping points. Choose based on how many models you're painting and where this unit sits in your army.

Tier 1

Tabletop Ready

~25–35 min per model
  • P-005 Dark Neutral Gray prime
  • Blood Red Speedpaint 2.0 — one-pass red armour
  • AMP 017 Red Orange drybrush highlight on armour edges
  • Coal Black 002 backpack and bolt rifle
  • Rich Gold 028 shoulder trim and icons (Agrax shade)
  • Khaki 061 purity seal parchment
  • One dot of Bright Jade 067 per lens
  • Basing complete (all 7 steps)
  • Matte varnish over all
  • Stop point: After the armour drybrush — reads solidly at arm's length from the table.
Tier 2

Battle Ready

~60–90 min per model
  • P-002 Black prime + Burgundy 069 shadow → Bold Pyrrole Red 003 → Red Tone wash → AMP 017 Red Orange highlight → Orange Red S31 edge highlight
  • Black weapons: Dark Neutral Grey 044 edge highlight
  • White Gold 029 edge highlight on gold trim
  • Full purity seal: Khaki → Brown Wash 201 → Bright Ivory 022 highlight + Coal Black 002 script suggestion
  • Full lens: Coal Black base → Bright Jade 067 → highlight → white specular dot + spot gloss
  • Basing complete
  • Matt varnish + spot gloss on lenses
  • Stop point: After edge highlights — looks deliberate and finished up close.
Tier 3

Display Quality

~3–6 hours per model
  • Everything in Tier 2, plus:
  • Shadow glaze pass — Burgundy 069 at 1:5 into lower panel thirds for deeper gradient
  • Consider OSL if any energy source is present (e.g. modded plasma weapons)
  • Black armour: second edge of Bright Neutral Grey 045 on apex corners
  • Wet blending on largest armour panels for smoother value transitions
  • Individual chapter icon detail: pick out relief sculpting on blood drop
  • Purity seal script: actual readable text strokes
  • Enhanced basing: second tuft type, cracked earth detail, environmental storytelling
  • Matt + targeted gloss on lenses, eyes, any blood/fluid effects
  • Stop point: When you're satisfied — there is no ceiling.
✦ ARMY ADVICE: For a 5-man squad, paint 4 to Tier 1 and your Sergeant or heaviest-posed model to Tier 2. The consistency across 4 models plus one standout reads better on the table than 5 models in different states of completion.

Brush Skills

Techniques used in this guide — practical workbench notes.

Edge Highlighting — Power Armour
Technique

Edge Highlighting Red Armour

Thin Orange Red S31 to 1:2 with water — it should flow off the brush tip with minimal pressure. Load a size 1 or 0 round, then wipe most of the paint off on a damp cloth until the brush holds only a trace. Touch the tip to a sharp armour edge and drag steadily. The paint follows the edge without pooling. Consistency comes from consistent brush pressure — light and even throughout the stroke. If it beads, thin slightly more. If it disappears, add slightly less water.

Drybrushing — Armour & Base
Technique

Drybrushing for Wargaming

Load your brush fully with AMP 017 Red Orange, then wipe firmly on a tissue or palette paper 8–10 strokes until almost no colour transfers. Test on your hand — a faint ghost of colour should appear, not a clear smear. Then lightly brush over armour edges and raised surfaces with quick multi-directional strokes. At 28mm, drybrushing is the single most efficient technique — it highlights every raised surface simultaneously in seconds. The key is a nearly-dry brush; too much paint and it reads muddy rather than highlighted.

Speedpaint 2.0 — One-Pass Application
Technique

Speedpaint One-Pass Rule

Load a medium round (size 2) with Blood Red Speedpaint 2.0 — no thinning, straight from the pot. Apply to a panel area, guiding the paint with the brush tip to cover the surface. The paint self-levels — let it flow into recesses naturally. Once you've covered an area, leave it. Do not go back and touch a wet area you've already applied. The self-leveling agent activates when the paint is disturbed while drying, causing streaks and tide lines. One deliberate pass per area is the discipline.

Wet Palette for Batch Consistency
Technique

Wet Palette — Batch of 5

Before starting any step that uses a mixed or thinned colour, load more paint onto the wet palette than you think you need — probably 3× more. Red Orange S31 edge highlighting across 5 models uses more paint than you'd expect per model. Using the same mix pool on all models means identical temperature and value across the batch. Between models, the palette keeps the mix workable. If the palette thins the mix over a long session, add a fresh drop of paint and re-blend rather than starting an entirely new mix.
