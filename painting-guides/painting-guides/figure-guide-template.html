Method Selection
[PLACEHOLDER: RECOMMENDED METHOD NAME] — [PLACEHOLDER: brief character analysis justifying the method choice.]

Method 1 — Basic

Classic layering. Prime → shadow undercoat → base coat → mid-tone → highlight → optional unifying glaze.

Most predictable; straightforward to correct Can read flat without enough layers Tabletop / 28mm, first-time painters, anime/cartoon (2–3 layers, no freckling)

Method 2A — Pinkle

Black prime → white zenithal → magenta all over → Transparent Red into recesses → thin flesh layers over top.

Luminous sub-surface scattering; fewer layers needed Poor zenithal = muddy result; harder to correct Fair / feminine, protagonist / hero figures, display 1:6 / 75mm / busts

Method 2B — Red Zenithal

Same as 2A but skip magenta — use Transparent Red 047 (1:2) or Flesh Wash directly over the white zenithal.

Natural warmth and exertion flush; works on darker complexions Same zenithal dependency as 2A Rugged male, military, survivor, weathered or world-worn figures
Freckling / Speckling: After skin is sealed, thin Burnt Sienna 059 or Red Orange 017 to near-watercolor. PSI 8–12, airbrush 3–5cm from surface, needle almost fully closed. Short trigger bursts — micro-dots, not lines. Target nose, cheeks, shoulders, backs of hands. One pass → step back → assess. Easy to overdo; practice on card first.

Step 5 · Finish

Cure — then matte varnish

Allow the effect product to cure for at least 2–3 hours (overnight is ideal) before applying your final matte varnish. Varnishing too early can reactivate the product and cause streaking or cloudiness.

⚠ Seal last: Apply matte varnish over the entire base including effect areas after curing. This locks everything in place and unifies the finish.
