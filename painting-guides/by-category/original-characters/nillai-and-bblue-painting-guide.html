Nillai & BBlue — A sci-fi original character built on contrast: soft luminous fair skin and casual lavender fabric against hard glossy teal mech armor. The candy teal metallic is the centrepiece — everything else serves it. Primary light is zenithal cool from above; warm fire OSL pushes from below through the thruster effects; teal OSL from BBlue's sensors and the base ring adds the sci-fi finish. Paint skin first to set the warm human anchor before committing to the cold mechanical tones.

Method Selection
RECOMMENDED: Method 2A — The Pinkle Method — Nillai is a heroic protagonist with fair warm skin and an idealized aesthetic. The Pinkle underpainting gives the skin a luminous "lit from within" quality that flat layering can't replicate, and it handles blush zones naturally. A strong zenithal is the foundation — invest time there.

Method 1 — Basic

Classic layering: prime → shadow undercoat → base coat → mid-tone → highlight → optional glaze.

Most predictable; easy to correct Can read flat at 1:6 without enough layers Speed priority, first-time painters, 28mm
Recommended

Method 2A — Pinkle

Black prime → white zenithal → magenta all over → Transparent Red into recesses → thin flesh layers over top.

Luminous sub-surface scattering; fewer flesh layers needed Poor zenithal = muddy result; harder to correct Fair feminine protagonist figures, display 1:6 / busts

Method 2B — Red Zenithal

Same as 2A but skip magenta — use Transparent Red directly over white zenithal, then flesh layers.

Natural warmth; works for darker or weathered complexions Same zenithal dependency as 2A Rugged male, military, weathered figures
Freckling / Speckling: After skin is sealed, thin Burnt Sienna 059 or Red Orange AMP-017 to near-watercolor. PSI 8–12, airbrush 3–5cm from surface, needle almost fully closed. Short bursts — micro-dots, not lines. Target nose, cheeks, shoulders, backs of hands. One pass → step back → assess. Practice on card first; it's very easy to overdo.

Magenta All-Over (The Pinkle)

Magenta 011 thinned 1:4. Semi-transparent coat over all skin — this is a tint, not coverage. The zenithal should remain clearly visible through it. 0.3mm nozzle, 12–15 PSI, 10–12cm. Two thin passes maximum.

✦ CANDY METALLIC PRINCIPLE: Semi-transparent teal glazes over a reflective silver base, sitting on gloss black. The gloss black is the permanent shadow — it shows through wherever light doesn't reach. You are painting the light that hits the metal, not coating the figure in teal paint. Restraint and multiple thin passes are the technique.
Foundation — Gloss Black Base
Step 1 · Airbrush

Gloss Black Base Coat

Satin Black S39 + MPAM-003 Gloss Varnish at 1:1, then thin the combined mix 1:2 with water. Apply over all armor surfaces with even coverage. Needs to be smooth and slightly glossy when dry. The gloss maximises reflectivity of the silver above. 0.3mm nozzle, 15–18 PSI, 10–15cm.

Silver Mapping — Light Planes
Step 2 · Airbrush

Silver — Lit Planes Only

Before spraying: hold the figure under your painting lamp and identify which armor planes face upward. Those get silver. Planes facing sideways or downward stay black — that shadow is already done. Spray Silver 025 thinned 1:3 from a zenithal angle, semi-transparent passes. Build over 2–3 passes on the most direct lit surfaces. Gloss black should still show through slightly on the first pass.

Silver 025
Pro Acryl
~65% value — metallic light layer; lit planes only
⚠ NOTE: Do NOT apply silver to the whole armor. The black base IS the shadow — covering it with silver kills the contrast that makes the candy effect work. Silver on lit planes only. Side planes partial. Downward or recessed planes stay black.
Candy Teal Color Glazes
Step 3 · Filter

Teal Candy Glaze — Color Build

Transparent Blue 046 + Turquoise 054 at 3:1 (blue-dominant to push toward teal rather than pure cyan). Thin to 1:4 to 1:5 — near-watercolor thin. Apply as multiple thin passes over the silver areas. Each pass adds color depth without obscuring the silver. Build until the teal reads rich and saturated — typically 4–6 passes. The silver bleeds through on lit surfaces giving the metallic quality.

Transparent Blue 046
Pro Acryl
deep transparent blue — candy base (3 parts)
Turquoise 054
Pro Acryl

Mix: 3:1 Transparent Blue 046 + Turquoise 054 — thin 1:4 to 1:5 paint:water
✦ TIP: After each pass, step back and assess under your lamp. You're aiming for a rich saturated teal — like an anodised aluminium surface or high-end automotive paint. The silver brightens the top-facing planes; the black darkens the side-facing ones. That natural difference creates the depth; the glazes add the color.
Step 4 · Filter (Optional)

Turbo Dork Effect Layer — Iridescent Depth

Turbo Dork Ice To Never thinned 1:1 with Turbo Dork Thinning Medium. Apply as a single thin pass over the dark (gloss black) planes only — not over the silver/teal areas. Ice To Never shifts between greenish-blue and purple at different viewing angles, giving the shadow zones a subtle iridescent depth. Applied over black, it activates fully. Applied over silver, it muddies the candy color.

BBlue — Companion Drone
Step 11 · Airbrush

BBlue — Teal Candy Recipe

BBlue shares Nillai's teal color family. Apply the same sequence: gloss black base → silver mapping on the upper hemisphere → candy teal glazes (Transparent Blue 046 + Turquoise 054, 3:1) → Bright Jade 067 edge highlights → Silver 025 apex edges → Bold Titanium White 001 specular dots. The drone is a sphere — the upper hemisphere lit, lower hemisphere dark split applies cleanly. Push the contrast hard; don't let the teal blend all the way around.

✦ TIP: The lit/shadow contrast on the sphere reads better at display distance if pushed harder than feels natural. Keep the underside near-black — the gloss black base makes this automatic if you stay disciplined about where you apply silver.
Step 12 · Brush

BBlue — Eye Sensor OSL

BBlue has two glowing white eye sensors. Base with Turquoise 054, then build to Bright Jade 067 in the outer ring, then Bold Titanium White 001 at the center — pure white is permitted for a glowing emitter. Then thin Turquoise 054 to 1:5 and glaze it onto the surrounding drone surface directly around each eye to simulate the teal-white OSL cast onto BBlue's own hull.

Armor & BBlue — Gloss Varnish

Candy Metallic Technique
✦ THE RULE: You are not painting the armor teal. You are painting the light that strikes the metal. The gloss black base is your permanent shadow. Silver maps where light lands. Teal glazes add color over the lit silver. Every pass should be restraint by default — you can always add, never remove.
Technique · Candy Check

Mid-Session Candy Assessment

After the second teal glaze pass, hold the figure under your lamp at arm's length. The lit surfaces should read as a distinct teal. The shadow surfaces should still read near-black. The transition between lit and shadow should be gradual but clearly visible. If the shadow zones are turning teal-grey, you've over-sprayed — stop and let cure. If the lit zones look flat and grey-green, the silver base wasn't reflective enough — pull back and gloss-varnish the surface, let cure, re-apply silver, then resume glazing.

OSL Airbrush Technique
Technique · Dual OSL

Painting Two Light Sources

This figure has two OSL systems — warm fire from below, cool teal from behind/side. Do them in separate sessions with a matte varnish seal between. Fire first (warm orange-yellow). Let cure fully. Matte varnish. Then teal OSL (cool cyan). This order prevents the warm and cool glazes from muddying each other. When positioning the airbrush for fire OSL, come from directly below — the thruster pushes upward and backward. For teal OSL from BBlue, come from the side the drone is positioned.

✦ TIP: Hold the figure over a dark surface while spraying OSL — any overspray lands on a dark background and you can see exactly how wide the spray pattern is before it hits the figure. Dial in distance and pressure before you commit to a pass.
Troubleshooting

Candy Effect Muddy / Not Popping

Cause: Gloss black base not glossy enough, or too many teal passes over shadow zones. Effect: Flat, dusty-looking teal rather than a metallic candy. Fix: Seal with MPAM-003 Gloss Varnish, let cure, then assess. If the silver shows no reflective depth, re-apply silver on lit planes and resume candy glazing from there.

OSL Too Bright / Kills Value Structure

Cause: OSL thinning too thick, or too many passes without checking. Effect: Surfaces near the light source read brighter than the figure's natural top-lighting; looks unnatural. Fix: There's no fix except repainting. Prevention: spray one pass, stop, assess at arm's length, then decide on a second. Each pass should be barely visible individually.

Tip Dry at Ultra-Thin Dilution

Cause: Paint dries at the nozzle tip when diluted to 1:6 or beyond. Effect: Spattering rather than a smooth cast; hard blobs on the figure. Fix: Pull needle back 1mm and blow through between each pass. Keep session time short. Add a drop of Glaze & Wash Medium MPAM-001 to slow drying at the tip.

Brush Skills

Techniques used in this guide: edge highlighting candy armor, freehand lightning cracks, eye detail work, OSL spot glazing, and metal recess lining.

Technique · Edge Loading

Armor Edge Highlighting

Load a size 1 round brush fully with thinned Bright Jade 067 (1:2). Wipe the brush almost dry on the palette edge, keeping paint only at the very tip. Run the tip along raised armor edges — pauldron rims, fin tops, plate corners. The brush should feel close to empty — the edge catch should be a near-dry stroke, not a visible paint deposit. For the Silver 025 metallic apex (armor Step 7), use the same approach with a 0 round brush: even more restrained, only the sharpest geometric corners.

✦ TIP: If the edge highlight looks strong from 30cm, it's too thick. It should be essentially invisible from 60cm and only legible at 20–30cm. Candy metallic armor reads from the overall light/shadow contrast, not from its edge lines.
Technique · Freehand Lightning Cracks

Confident Short Strokes

The lightning crack pattern on the purple fabric is freehand with a 10/0 liner brush. The technique is confident short strokes (2–4mm) pulled in direction, connected at angles. Do not try to plan the whole path first — start from a branching point, pull short, angle, pull short again. Gaps read as intentional; uniform smooth lines read as mechanical. Do the grey base lines first, let dry, then trace the white center through the center of each grey line. Practice 4–5 crack clusters on scrap card before committing to the figure.

✦ TIP: Rest your hand against the figure's base or your work surface for stability. Liner brushes are long and responsive — even a small hand tremor reads on the figure. Ground yourself physically before each stroke.
Technique · Eye Detail

10/0 Eye Control

At 1:6 scale the eye socket is approximately 6–7mm wide. Work in a bright light with magnification. The painting sequence is: white sclera → iris color → pupil dot → limbal ring thin arc → iris catchlight dot → white catchlight → gloss varnish. Each step waits for the previous to dry fully before proceeding. The limbal ring (thin dark arc at the top of the iris under the upper lid) is the single highest-impact detail — it creates lid shadow and perceived depth with one thin stroke. Work both eyes simultaneously so the catchlight position matches.

Technique · OSL Spot Glazing

OSL Over Sealed Surfaces

Brush-applied OSL requires a sealed surface to prevent lifting. Thin the glaze color (Transparent Orange 050 for fire; Turquoise 054 for teal) to 1:6 to 1:8. Load a soft flat brush, wick off excess on the palette, then lay the loaded face of the brush onto the surface — not a stroke, a press-and-lift. The pressure releases the thin layer evenly without brushmarks. Work from the light source outward, progressively reducing amount with each subsequent zone. Let each press dry before the next.

Technique · Metal Recess Lining

Precision Wash Placement

Metal panel lines and joint gaps require targeted wash placement, not flood washing. Load a 10/0 round brush with Black Wash 200 thinned 1:1 with MPAM-001 Glaze & Wash Medium. Touch the tip to the entry point of a panel line and let capillary action draw the wash along the gap. Do not drag the brush — let surface tension work. If the wash runs onto a flat panel surface, blot immediately with a clean damp brush (not wiped — pressed flat and lifted). Work one panel cluster at a time.
