Hive Fleet Leviathan Termagants — the quintessential Tyranid swarm in the most iconic bio-form scheme: deep purple carapace shifting to hot magenta at the plate edges, set against near-white bone flesh. At 28mm, value contrast does all the heavy lifting — rich near-black in the deepest plate recesses and pure near-white on the sharpest bone ridges is what makes this scheme sing across the tabletop. Zenithal priming locks in value structure before a single color touches the model. Light source is zenithal (from directly above). Priority order: carapace first (the defining read), then flesh, then claws, then details.

P-003 White Primer undiluted, 0.5mm nozzle, 30+ PSI. Aim directly from above — top surfaces get full white, sides get partial, undersides stay black. This establishes your full value gradient before color is applied. At 28mm, a strong zenithal is what prevents flat-looking miniatures.

Technique · Batch Flow

Assembly-Line Airbrush Workflow

Mount all models on cork or bluetack holders before starting. Complete each airbrush step across ALL models before moving to the next step. Prime all → zenithal all → Dark Purple all → Purple all → Magenta all → set down airbrush. Then brush steps the same way: wash all → edge all → specular all. Switching colors multiple times per model is the slowest possible approach. The purple gradient (steps 3–5) is the most impactful series. Even if you skip brush edge highlights, three airbrush passes give a complete, professional-looking carapace.

✦ TIP: Batch size of 10 is the sweet spot. Larger batches mean paint dries on the needle before you finish the row. If you're painting 30+ Termagants, work in groups of 10.
Troubleshooting

Blowback / Bubbling

Cause: Too much air, trigger pressed fully before pulling back, or paint cup too full. Fix: Reduce PSI by 5, pull trigger back slowly while air is already flowing. Never fill the cup more than 2/3. Partially close needle while at rest between passes.

Uneven Coverage / Banding

Cause: Moving the airbrush too slowly, stopping mid-stroke, or inconsistent trigger pressure. Fix: Keep moving at constant speed. Start and end each stroke off the model. Practice on scrap card before applying to figures.

Paint Too Thin / Runs

Cause: Over-thinned paint or too many passes in one spot at 28mm scale. Fix: Test each mix on card — it should flow off a palette knife in a slow, unbroken ribbon. At 28mm, 1:2 is usually the maximum dilution for base coats. Add paint back to your mix if coverage is lost.

Brush Skills

Edge highlighting, targeted washing, drybrushing, and fine detail work — the four brush techniques that carry this guide.

Technique · Edge Highlighting

Edge Highlighting at 28mm

Load a size 0 or 00 round brush with thinned paint (1:2). Wipe excess on palette until the brush barely deposits color. Drag the tip along raised edges in one confident, continuous stroke. At 28mm the highlight line should be 0.3–0.5mm wide — visible but not fat. Hesitant strokes create wobbly highlights. Commit to the stroke and let it flow. Rest your brush hand's little finger on the model or a nearby surface for stability. Practice on a spare base or sprue first.

✦ TIP: Work across all models in the batch before stopping. Consistency of pressure and speed produces more uniform results than perfecting one model before moving to the next.
Technique · Targeted Wash

Recess Washing — Capillary Control

A correctly applied wash flows into recesses via capillary action without puddling on flat surfaces. Load the brush but don't overload — touch the tip to a recess and lift. The wash flows in on its own. Do not brush it around. If it puddles on a raised surface, pick up excess immediately with a clean, slightly damp brush. Thin washes 1:1 with water for tight 28mm recesses — full-strength wash is too viscous and leaves tide marks.

Technique · Drybrushing

Drybrushing for Organic Texture

Load a stiff, wide brush (an old flat brush worn down works perfectly). Wipe on cloth or tissue until almost no paint transfers — the brush should feel nearly dry. Skim lightly over the model surface. The bristles catch only the highest raised points. Two-pass approach for Termagant flesh: firm drybrush with Bright Ivory 022, then very light final drybrush with Heavy Warm White S18 on absolute peaks only.

⚠ NOTE: A wet or overloaded drybrush covers everything and destroys value structure. When in doubt, wipe more. The goal is delicate deposition, not heavy coverage.
Technique · Fine Eye Detail

Eye Detail at 28mm

At 28mm the eye is 0.5–1mm across. Use a freshly pointed size 000 brush. Two methods: (1) Dry-tip — wipe brush near-dry, press just the very tip to the surface. (2) For specular hot spot dots — a toothpick tip works better than any brush. Dip in thinned Orange 007, touch to eye, done. Always paint in order: dark socket → base color → glow color → hot spot. Never try to add a dark ring around a painted eye at this scale — it creates a muddy mess.

✦ TIP: The gloss varnish dot on the eye is the most impactful single brush touch on the whole model. Do not skip it.

Grimdark Weathering

The Tyranid are a living weapon system. Nothing about them is clean or manufactured — they are grown, and living things are messy. Ichor seeps where carapace plates meet living flesh. Spore colonies establish themselves on the carapace surface during transit. The biomass the Hive Fleet has been consuming for six thousand years has left traces on every organism it grows. These techniques lean into the biological horror rather than the mechanical grime of Space Marine weathering. Two paths: Villainy Ink enamel or John Blanche Masterclass acrylic.

⚠ ENAMEL WARNING — READ BEFORE OPENING A VILLAINY INK:
Villainy Inks are oil/enamel-based — they are NOT acrylic. Applied to bare or unsealed acrylic paint they will soften and lift it. You must seal the model with varnish before any Villainy Ink step. Cleanup is with enamel thinner or white spirit only. Dry time: 4–12 hours. Keep a dedicated enamel-only brush and never rinse it in your acrylic water cup.
Choose Your Path
☠ Path A — Enamel

Villainy Ink Path

The Tyranid model is unusually well-suited to the enamel path because the biological detail — the carapace-to-flesh joins, the spore chimneys, the weapon symbiote interfaces — creates natural recess structures for the enamel to pool in. VI-004 Carrion Crimson at the flesh joins is the centrepiece step: it reads as ichor, haemolymph, and the general wet biological interface between a living creature's hard and soft tissues.

You'll need: MPAR-V01 Matte Varnish, VI-004 Carrion Crimson, VI-005 Ichor of the Damned, VI-006 Decay of Death, VI-001 Goon's Grime, enamel thinner, cotton swabs, dedicated enamel brush.

🖌 Path B — Acrylic

John Blanche Masterclass Path

All acrylic, no seal required. The biological effects are achieved with WP4008 Grimdark Shadow for general grime, Army Painter Blood Effects for the ichor, and WP4013 for additional biological staining. Skip to the John Blanche Acrylic Path section below.

You'll need: WP4008 Grimdark Shadow, WP3164 Blood Effects Acrylic, WP4005 Grime Wash.

Trouble Shooting
Enamel lifted the carapace or flesh paint
Cause: Applied Villainy Ink to an unsealed surface.
Fix: Let the enamel dry fully (4–12 hours). Touch up the carapace with Violet 010 / Ultramarine Blue equivalent; touch up flesh with the original flesh base colours. Seal thoroughly before any further enamel work. The seal step is not optional.
Carrion Crimson flooded onto the carapace plate, not just the join
Cause: Applied too much ink, or didn't remove it from the plate surface quickly enough.
Fix: Re-wet with a cotton swab barely dampened with enamel thinner and drag it across the carapace plate face — the ink lifts off flat surfaces and stays in the join groove. If the enamel has dried, re-wet the surface, wait 30 seconds, then wipe with a clean swab. The join line effect only works if the plate surface itself is clean.
Decay of Death spore spots look like painted dots, not organic
Cause: Applied with a brush stroke rather than a stipple press, or made the spots too uniform in size.
Fix: Vary the spot sizes — some as small as a pinpoint, some 1–2mm, placed in irregular clusters of 3–5. Between spots, add one or two smaller satellite marks nearby. Real fungal/bacterial colonies are never perfectly uniform. The irregularity is what reads as biological rather than decorative.
Gloss flesh finish was dulled by the matte varnish seal coat
Cause: Applied matte varnish over the whole model before restoring the flesh gloss.
Fix: This is expected — the varnish seal step intentionally flattens everything. After the enamel inks are fully cured and the matte seal coat is dry, apply MPAM-003 Gloss Varnish with a fine brush specifically over all flesh surfaces and the eye lenses. The gloss finish on the flesh against matte carapace is a critical visual contrast — restore it last.
