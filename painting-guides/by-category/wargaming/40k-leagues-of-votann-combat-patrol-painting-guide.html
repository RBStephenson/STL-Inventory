Batch Painting Workflow

The Combat Patrol box contains Hearthkyn Warriors, an Einhyr Champion, Hernkyn Pioneers, and a Sagitaur — a healthy mix of infantry, character, and vehicle. Treat the Champions and Pioneers as Tier 2 models and the rank Hearthkyn as your Tier 1 batch.

Recommended Batch Sizes
✦ ARMY COHESION: Lock in your base recipe before touching the first model. The tundra base theme must be consistent across everything — infantry, vehicle, and character alike. A unified base is the single biggest factor in making a mixed box look like a deliberate army.
Tier 1 — Speedpaint Shortcut (Hearthkyn Warriors)
✦ PURPLE PRIMER TRICK: Prime with MPAP-023 Dark Purple airbrush primer instead of grey. Then apply Speedpaint 2.0 Purple Swarm directly over it — the dark purple primer reads through recesses as a deeper shadow, giving you a richer, more saturated result in one pass with zero extra effort.
Assembly-Line Sequence

Complete each stage across ALL models before moving to the next. Never finish one model before touching the others.

1
Prep & Prime — All Models

Clean mold lines. Prime all infantry with MPAP-023 Dark Purple via airbrush (0.5mm, 28 PSI, full coverage). Prime the Sagitaur with MPAR-P02 Matte Black spray as a base for its larger surfaces. Allow 30 min dry time before proceeding.

2
Purple Armour — All Infantry

Tier 1 (fast): Speedpaint 2.0 Purple Swarm over all armour — one pass, no reworking while wet. Let flow into recesses and leave it. Tier 2+ (airbrushed): See the Purple Armour tab for the full zenithal workflow. Do whichever you've chosen across the entire batch before moving on.

3
Brass Trim — All Models

Apply Dark Bronze S24 as a base coat over all brass trim, runes, and armour borders — all models. Then Rich Gold 028 over raised trim — all models. Then Brown Wash 201 targeted into brass recesses — all models. Do not move to the next colour until all models have had the full brass sequence.

4
Leather, Cloth & Bodysuit — All Infantry

Dark Umber 019 on all leather straps and pouches → all models. Dark Grey Blue 014 on bodysuit under-armour sections → all models. One colour at a time across the entire batch.

5
Highlight Pass — All Models

Drybrush Faded Plum 063 over all raised armour surfaces and edges — all models. This is the Tier 1 stopping point for the armour. Drybrush Bright Gold 031 over raised brass edges — all models. Tier 2 edge highlights come after this, still across the batch.

6
Details & Ancestor Glow — All Models

Ancestor glow (helmet optics, core ports, weapon cells) — all models in sequence: dark teal base → mid teal → bright Turquoise 054 hot spot → all models. Then eyes → all models. Then weapon steel accents → all models. One detail type at a time, always across the full batch.

7
Basing — All Models Together

Apply texture paste to all bases. Prime, colour, shade, drybrush, frost — all models at each stage. Never base one model while others are mid-paint. See the Basing tab for the complete tundra recipe.

8
Varnish — All Models at Once

Matte varnish overall (MPAR-V01 Matte Varnish spray or brush-on equivalent). Then targeted gloss on ancestor glow areas, eye lenses, and any gem surfaces. The glow needs that gloss to read correctly — don't skip it.

Sagitaur Vehicle Notes
✦ SAGITAUR: Same colour recipe scales directly to the vehicle. Key differences: highlight passes are more aggressive on large flat panels (bigger drybrush strokes, more passes); add weathering streaks using Brown Wash 201 drawn downward from vents and panel gaps; the ancestor core housing on the vehicle is a great opportunity for a strong OSL glow effect — see the Ancestor Glow tab for the approach.
Wet Palette Protocol
✦ WET PALETTE: When brush-painting across a batch, a wet palette is non-negotiable. Mix enough of each colour to cover the entire batch in one session — err on the side of too much. The same pool = the same colour on every model. Never mix "just enough" for one model.

Purple Armour

The dominant surface — dense Votann combat armour in deep royal purple. The goal is a rich, saturated purple that holds darkness in the recesses while catching teal ancestor-light on the edges at Tier 2+. Values should read as 20–25% in shadows, 50–55% on mid panels, with edge highlights pushing to 70–75%.

Tier 1 — Speedpaint Fast Track
✦ TIER 1 START HERE: Prime with MPAP-023 Dark Purple (full coverage, let cure 30 min). Apply Speedpaint 2.0 Purple Swarm in a single flowing pass — work from top to bottom, guide the paint into recesses with the brush tip, then leave it. Do NOT rework while wet. Let dry completely (20 min minimum). Follow with Step 5 (drybrush) to complete Tier 1.

Brass, Leather & Cloth

The secondary materials carry the 30% of the scheme not covered by purple armour. Aged brass dominates trim and equipment housings; dark leather handles straps and pouches; a very dark blue-grey bodysuit sits beneath the armour and provides visual separation without competing with the purple.

Ancestor Glow

The teal ancestor-light is the defining accent of the Leagues of Votann. It appears on helmet optics, core port indicators, weapon power cells, and Kin marker runes. At Tier 1 it's a simple two-step basecoat; at Tier 2+ it becomes the miniature's most striking feature with layered OSL. This is worth the effort — teal against deep purple is a textbook split-complement contrast.

Key Glow Locations
✦ INVENTORY: Identify all ancestor glow areas before painting anything. Common locations: helmet visor displays, core port rings on chestplate, weapon power lines, markings on wrist-bracer displays, and Ancestor Core housing on the Sagitaur. Painting them all to a consistent brightness ties the army together.

Basing

Frozen tundra — cracked grey earth with frost accumulation on raised surfaces and sparse dead vegetation. The cold grey-white basing temperature contrasts against the warm purple armour and brass trim, making both pop. Build and paint all bases before starting the figures.

✦ ARMY RULE: Base all models together before painting figures. Every base must match — infantry, character, and Sagitaur alike. Lock in this recipe before touching paint on the first Hearthkyn.
Base Theme: Frozen Tundra
Step 1 · Base Texture

Apply Concrete Texture

Apply MPA-T05 Concrete texture paste with an old stiff brush — cover 70–80% of the base surface, leaving a few small flat spots near the model's feet for stability. Push the texture into edges. While still wet, press in one or two small pieces of gravel or sprue nub for rock variety. Let cure 2+ hours before priming. Do not rush this dry time — inadequately cured texture flakes when primed.

✦ TIP: Build the texture up in the centre and thin it toward the rim — this prevents pooling paint at the edges and gives a natural terrain appearance.
Step 2 · Prime / Seal

Lock the Texture — Dark Grey Prime

MPAP-005 Dark Neutral Grey via airbrush (0.5mm, 28 PSI) or MPAR-P02 Matte Black spray over all bases. Full coverage — this seals the texture paste, prevents flaking, and establishes the deepest value in the crevices. All bases at once.

Step 3 · Base Colour

Cold Ground Colour

Dark Warm Grey 016 thinned 1:2. Brush over all texture surfaces, leaving the primer colour in the deepest crevices. This cool-warm mid-grey reads as frozen, rocky earth — neither warm brown nor pure cool grey. Apply to all bases before proceeding.

Dark Warm Grey 016
Pro Acryl
~22% value — frozen ground colour
Step 4 · Shade

Deepen the Crevices

Black Wash 200 thinned 1:2, worked into all texture crevices, rock shadows, and the base perimeter edge. Ties the texture together and pushes the shadows deeper. Allow to dry completely before drybrushing.

Black Wash 200
Pro Acryl
~5% value — deep crevice shade
Step 5 · Drybrush

Cold Surface Highlight — Two Passes

Pass 1 (mid highlight): Bright Warm Grey 015 thinned slightly or used undiluted — wipe brush dry, random multi-directional strokes over all raised texture peaks and gravel surfaces. Builds the primary surface colour. Should reach approximately 55% value on the highest points. Pass 2 (frost edge): Mix Bright Warm Grey 015 + Heavy Warm White S18 at 2:1, very light touch, catching only the absolute topmost peaks. Reads as frost accumulation on exposed edges.

Bright Warm Grey 015
Pro Acryl
~55% value — mid surface highlight
Heavy Warm White S18
Pro Acryl

Step 6 · Detail Elements

Frost Texture & Tufts

Optional frost accumulation effect: mix PVA glue with a small amount of Heavy Warm White S18 and Bright Ivory 022 (3:1:1 PVA to each paint). Stipple sparingly onto exposed rock edges, the windward side of gravel, and around base perimeter edges — simulating blown frost settling on surfaces. For tufts: press sparse dead-grass or frost tufts into a small dot of PVA glue. Place one or two per base maximum — tundra is sparse, not overgrown.

Base Rim — Coal Black

Coal Black 002 undiluted, painted around the full circumference of every base rim. A consistent black rim frames the model and keeps the eye on the figure. This is non-negotiable across every model in the army — no exceptions, no variations.

Quality Tiers

Three tracks — same base recipe, different stopping points. For the Combat Patrol, consider painting Hearthkyn Warriors to Tier 1, Hernkyn Pioneers to Tier 2, and your Einhyr Champion to Tier 3.

Tier 1

Tabletop Ready

~25–35 min/model at pace
  • Prime MPAP-023 Dark Purple → Speedpaint Purple Swarm one-pass over all armour
  • Speedpaint Golden Armour over brass trim (one-pass)
  • Dark Umber 019 on leather, Dark Grey Blue 014 on bodysuit
  • Drybrush Faded Plum 063 over all armour edges
  • Speedpaint Plasmatic Bolt over all ancestor glow areas (one-pass over Coal Black base)
  • Crusader Skin one-pass on exposed skin
  • Weapon steel: Coal Black 002 + Dark Silver 030 base → Steel 010 drybrush
  • Full basing recipe; matte varnish; spot gloss on glow areas
  • Stop point: After drybrush and ancestor glow — reads as a complete, cohesive army model at arm's length.
Tier 2

Battle Ready

~55–80 min/model
  • Everything in Tier 1, plus:

Brush Skills

Techniques used in this guide — practical notes for the workbench.

Edge Highlighting
Technique

Edge Highlighting on Votann Armour

Thin your highlight paint to 1:2 — it should flow off the brush with minimal pressure. Load a size 1 or size 0 round brush and barely touch the tip to a sharp armour edge, then draw steadily along its length. The paint deposits by capillary action along the edge. The Votann armour panels are well-defined with clear borders — follow these edges rather than the sculpt texture. Practice the steadiness on a Hearthkyn sprue offcut before committing to the champion. If the line is too wide, the paint is too thin; if it skips, it's too thick.

Drybrushing
Technique

Drybrushing for Armour & Basing

Load your brush with undiluted paint, then wipe aggressively on tissue until almost no colour transfers — test on the back of your hand, you should see only the faintest trace. Then use quick multi-directional strokes over raised surfaces. For Votann armour: light pressure, broad strokes across all panels at once. For basing: random strokes in all directions to simulate frost depositing uniformly on rocks. A stiff, splayed brush is ideal. Multiple very light passes give more control than one heavy stroke. The goal on armour is caught edges; on basing it is uniformly frosted peaks.

Speedpaint 2.0 One-Pass
Technique

Using Speedpaint Correctly

Speedpaint 2.0 is a one-pass product. Load your brush, apply to the surface, and use the brush tip to guide the paint into recesses. Then set the brush down and walk away. The paint self-levels into recesses as it dries — this is the entire point. Any reworking while wet causes streaks, patchiness, and loss of the recess shading. If you made a mistake, let it dry completely, then correct over the top. Thin only with Speedpaint Medium — not water. Water breaks the reactivation chemistry.

OSL Glazing
Technique

Applying the Ancestor Glow OSL

OSL (Object Source Lighting) glazing works best over a fully dried and sealed surface — apply a mid-session matte varnish before you glaze. Thin Turquoise 054 at 1:5 with Glaze & Wash Medium MPAM-001 for the smoothest result. Load a soft flat brush and apply with very light pressure — you're depositing a tint, not painting over. Work in a small radius around the glow source: the glaze should be strongest right at the edge of the glow area and fade to nothing within 3–4mm. Wipe back any pooling immediately with a barely damp flat brush. Build up two thin passes rather than one thick one. The result should read as a suggestion of teal light, not an obvious paint application.

Wet Palette — Batch Consistency
Technique

Wet Palette for a 10-Model Batch

Mix at least 50% more of each colour than you think you need — mixes on a wet palette thin slightly over a long session. Keep all active mixes on the palette simultaneously so you can switch between models fluidly. Between picking up paint for a new model, check the consistency — if the mix has thinned on the palette, remix from fresh paint to restore it. Same consistency = same colour across all ten models. Never mix "just enough" for one model and remix fresh for the next. The slight inconsistency between mixes is where army cohesion dies.

Common Challenges
Purple wash pooling on flat armour panels
Thin the wash further (try 1:2 with water) and apply targeted with a fine brush rather than flooding the surface. For large flat areas, wick excess away immediately with a dry brush tip before it pools.
Brass trim looks too bright yellow, not aged
The Brown Wash 201 is doing the ageing — if it looks too yellow, apply a second targeted pass of Brown Wash into the recesses and as a thin glaze over the broad surfaces. Then re-apply Bright Gold 031 edges only after drying.
Ancestor glow looks flat and painted-on, not glowing
Value contrast is the issue — the dark base needs to be darker (Coal Black 002, not grey), and the centre needs to be brighter (push to Bright Jade 067 + Heavy Warm White S18 at the hot tip). The glow reads best when the dark-to-light range spans at least 10% to 70% value. Also ensure you've applied spot gloss varnish over the glow after matte varnishing.
Speedpaint Purple Swarm looks streaky after drying
This happens when the paint was reworked while wet. Speedpaint cannot be corrected while drying — let it dry fully, then apply a thin glaze of Transparent Purple 051 thinned 1:4 to unify the surface. Follow with the drybrush step to visually break up any remaining unevenness.
