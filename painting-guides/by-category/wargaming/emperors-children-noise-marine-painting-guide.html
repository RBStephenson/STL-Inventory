Noise Marines are the sonic-weapon warriors of the Emperor's Children, a Chaos Space Marine Legion devoted entirely to Slaanesh — the Chaos God of excess, sensation, and pleasure. Once among the Emperor's finest, they now wage war as an act of devotion, their Blastmasters and Sonic Blasters tuned to frequencies that liquefy organs and shatter souls. The Noise Marine Champion leads the squad, distinguished by elaborate wargear and even more extreme bodily modification. Painting priority: the purple-to-pink armour gradient is the defining visual — establish the full dark-to-vivid-pink value range before touching any other element. Gold trim is ostentatious and ceremonial, not functional. Sonic weapon emitters glow hot pink — the only true OSL element on the model.

Slaanesh Purple Power Armour

The Emperor's Children wear purple and gold — the same palatial colours they carried as one of the Emperor's finest Legions, now steeped in ten thousand years of Slaaneshi excess. The purple armour transitions from near-black in the recesses to hot pink at the highest edges, mimicking the gradient of bruised flesh and the eye of a predator. Value first: establish the dark-to-pink progression before worrying about exact hue.

Step 1 · Airbrush

Full Black Prime — P-002

Prime the entire model with Pro Acryl P-002 Black Primer. Undiluted, 0.5mm nozzle, 30+ PSI, 20–25cm distance. Full coverage — every recess, every undercut, every gap. This is the value floor: 5% dark, the deepest shadow anchor. Never thin primer.

P-002 Black Primer
Pro Acryl Primer
~5% value — full coverage base; never thin primer
Step 2 · Airbrush

Dark Purple Shadow — Zenithal Build

Thin Dark Purple 035 to 1:2 and apply from a 45° angle above, covering the upper half of all armour surfaces. This is the shadow base colour at ~20% value — it sits between the pure-black recesses and the main purple layer above. Keep the black fully intact in all deep recesses, corner shadows, and any area that would be in shade from the zenithal light source. Two passes, let each flash-dry 30 seconds.

Dark Purple 035
Pro Acryl
~20% value — shadow purple; zenithal from 45°; preserve black in deepest recesses
Step 3 · Airbrush

True Purple Mid-Tone — Main Colour Layer

Thin Purple 010 to 1:2.5 and apply from directly above (zenithal). This is the true purple mid-tone at ~42% value — the foundational colour that the eye reads as "purple plate." It should fully cover the raised panel faces and upper armour surfaces while leaving the Dark Purple 035 intact on all shadow areas. Two to three passes. At 28mm this colour covers a lot of ground quickly — check after each pass.

Purple 010
Pro Acryl
~42% value — main purple mid-tone; raised faces and upper surfaces; zenithal from directly above
Step 4 · Airbrush

Plum Highlight — Lifted Mid-Tone

Thin Plum 070 to 1:3 and apply in a tight zenithal pattern from directly above. This lifts the value on the uppermost plate surfaces to ~62% — noticeably lighter than the Purple 010 beneath, beginning the dark-to-pink value journey. Restrict the trigger to keep this highlight contained to raised armour faces only. It should not extend down into panel joins or recesses.

Plum 070
Pro Acryl
~62% value — lifted highlight; restrict to raised plate faces only; tight zenithal
Step 5 · Airbrush

Pink Edge Pre-Highlight — Zenith Peak

Thin Pink 071 to 1:4 — very thin — and apply from directly above with a very restricted trigger, hitting only the very apex of raised plate surfaces. This is the ~80% value pink pre-highlight that the brush edge highlights will tie into. At 28mm this application is small. If you have a 0.2mm nozzle, use it here. If not, keep the trigger barely cracked and move quickly across the surface from above.

Pink 071
Pro Acryl
~80% value — pink zenith pre-highlight; apex of raised surfaces only; very restricted trigger
Step 6 · Brush

Pale Pink Edge Highlights

Thin Pale Pink 043 to 1:1 and apply to all armour edges with a size 0 detail brush. The pale pink edge line sits at ~90% value — near-white with a delicate pink temperature. Every raised armour panel edge gets this treatment: pauldron rims, torso panel seams, knee guard edges, helmet crest. The line should be thin and consistent — one brush-width at most. This step ties together the zenithal pre-highlight and gives the model its finished look.

Pale Pink 043
Pro Acryl
~90% value — edge highlight; all armour panel edges; size 0 brush; one confident stroke per edge
Step 7 · Brush

Final Specular — One Dot of Near-White

Mix Heavy Warm White S18 with a tiny amount of Pale Pink 043 (4:1). Apply a single dot to the highest corner or apex of each armour plate — the absolute brightest point at ~94% value. One dot per plate face. This is the specular highlight that catches the eye across the table. Do not over-apply — if every plate face gets a large specular, none of them read as the hottest point. Reserve it for the most prominent surfaces: upper pauldrons, helmet dome, chest plate crown.

Heavy Warm White S18 + Pale Pink 043 (4:1)

Step 8 · Wash

Panel Line Depth — Targeted Black Wash

Using a fine liner brush, apply Pro Acryl Black Wash 200 (thinned 1:2) into the panel line channels across all armour plates. Touch the brush to the recess opening and let capillary action pull the wash in. Do not flood plate faces. This recovers any panel line definition lost during the airbrush layers and deepens the shadow at all armour joins. The dark wash at the bottom of panel lines contrasts with the pink edge highlights immediately above them — the visual tension between deep shadow and bright edge is what reads as "3D armour" at arm's length.

This establishes the shadow value at ~15% — dark enough to read as a shadow anchor while maintaining purple temperature. Two coats for opacity. Get full coverage; this is the foundation everything else builds on.

Mid-tone Layer — Purple 010

Thin Purple 010 to 1:1.5. Apply to all raised armour panel faces, leaving the dark base mix in all recesses, panel joins, and contact points between armour surfaces. This is the main colour at ~42% value. Build in two thin layers — the first pass establishes coverage, the second refines the boundary between shadow and mid-tone. Think in terms of "where would light actually hit this armour piece?" — paint only those surfaces.

Gold Trim & Slaanesh Iconography

The Emperor's Children's gold is ceremonial — not the utilitarian gold of the Salamanders or the ancient arcane gold of the Thousand Sons. It is the gold of a Legion that worships excess: bright, ostentatious, ornate. Every surface the EC have gold on, it was put there deliberately. The Slaanesh six-pointed star, musical motifs, scrollwork, skull crests — these elements define the unit at arm's length. Paint the gold with the same care you'd give the armour.

Step 1 · Airbrush

VMC 77.725 Gold — Base Metallic Layer

Thin Vallejo Metal Color 77.725 Gold 1:1 with distilled water. Apply to all gold trim surfaces from above using a 0.3mm nozzle. Agitate the paint cup every 2–3 minutes — VMC metallic particles settle quickly. Two passes for a solid metallic base. At this stage the gold looks raw and unfinished — that's correct; the wash and highlights complete it. Apply over the black prime; the VMC metallic coverage is excellent over dark backgrounds.

Brown Wash 201 — Gold Recess Shadow

Apply Pro Acryl Brown Wash 201 undiluted or at 1:1 into all gold recess lines — where trim meets armour plate, the inside curve of decorative elements, scroll bases. The warm-brown wash darkens the gold recesses to ~30% value and gives the trim an aged, warm shadow — it stops the gold looking flat and uniform.

Slaanesh Star, Scrollwork & Musical Motifs

Emperor's Children armour is covered in Slaanesh iconography: the six-pointed Chaos star with the Slaanesh crescent at its centre, scrollwork inscriptions, lute and harp motifs, and the occasional stylised face or mask. Treat every iconographic element as a miniature gold painting exercise: Dark Umber shadow in every recess of the star, Rich Gold on the flat faces, Bright Gold on the raised points of the star's tips. The six points of the Slaanesh star should each get a White Gold specular dot at their very tip — this makes the star sparkle and read clearly at 28mm. Any bone or ivory panels on the armour (chest eagle, scrolls) follow the standard bone recipe from the Base tab.

Sonic Weapons — Blastmaster & Sonic Blaster

The sonic weapons of the Emperor's Children are unique in the 40k universe — part weapon, part instrument, entirely Slaanesh. They have three distinct material zones that need painting: the dark steel mechanical body, the ivory/bone speaker horns (reminiscent of brass instrument bells), and the pink-magenta sonic energy field at the emitter. These three zones — dark metal, warm bone, vivid pink — create maximum contrast in a small space and define the model's character.

Step 1 · Brush

Weapon Body — Dark Steel

The mechanical chassis and grip of the sonic weapon is treated as dark armoured metal — the same palette as a boltgun but with EC purple accent panels. Base the weapon body with Coal Black 002 + Dark Neutral Grey 044 (2:1). Apply VMC 77.720 Gunmetal Grey to the main metal surfaces with a size 1 brush, leaving the Coal Black mix in all recesses and joins. Edge highlight with Silver 025 on the weapon's outer chassis corners. The weapon body should be substantially darker than the armour — it's utilitarian machinery compared to the ceremonial plate.

Step 3 · Brush

Speaker Horns — Bone to Ivory Gradient

The speaker horns and emitter bells are the most distinctive visual element of sonic weapons — they look like brass instrument bells repurposed as weapons of mass destruction. Paint them as warm bone: Dark Umber 019 base → Khaki 061 mid-tone layer on raised horn faces → Bright Ivory 022 highlight on horn rim edges and outer bell face → final edge line of Heavy Warm White S18 at the bell lip (the thinnest part of the horn, where real brass instruments have their brightest highlight). The gradient should be darkest at the horn throat and brightest at the bell opening. Apply Army Painter Fanatic Strong Tone wash thinned 1:2 into the horn throat to deepen the shadow at the deepest point.

Sonic Energy Field — Pink Magenta Glow

The emitter face and any sonic discharge energy on the weapon gets a hot pink-to-magenta OSL (Object Source Lighting) treatment. Base the emitter recess with Coal Black 002. Apply Magenta 011 as the energy field colour — undiluted in the centre of the emitter, thinned 1:3 as a glaze spreading outward toward the emitter rim. Apply Pink 071 as a brighter inner zone at the very centre. Finish with a single Pale Pink 043 dot at the absolute centre — the hottest point of the sonic field. Seal the entire emitter face with MPAM-003 Gloss. The contrast between the dark weapon body and the vivid gloss-pink energy field is the defining visual statement of the Noise Marine.

Step 5 · Brush — Optional

OSL — Sonic Energy Bounce Light on Armour

Optional but very effective: apply a subtle pink OSL (object source lighting) effect onto the armour surfaces immediately surrounding the sonic weapon emitter. Thin Magenta 011 to 1:5 and glaze very lightly over the Purple 010 armour surfaces directly adjacent to the weapon — the weapon hand, the nearest chest plate, the lower pauldron interior. The wash shifts those nearby surfaces subtly toward pink, implying the sonic energy field is genuinely emitting coloured light. Apply in one pass and leave it — the subtlety is the point. If it's visible but you have to look for it, you've applied exactly the right amount.

Step 6 · Brush

Gold EC Weapon Iconography

Any gold trim on the weapon — decorative bands around the horn, Slaanesh stars on the housing, ornate grip scrollwork — follows the same gold recipe from the Gold & Iconography tab. On a sonic weapon these elements should feel deliberately ornate and musical: imagine a jewelled instrument as much as a weapon. Even the smallest decorative dot gets a Rich Gold base and Bright Gold highlight — at 28mm these details are tiny but they read correctly as ornamentation.

Flesh & Excess

The Emperor's Children are defined by physical excess — they have modified, pierced, decorated, and exposed their own bodies as acts of devotion to Slaanesh. Where other Space Marines' flesh is hidden, EC show it. Their skin tones lean slightly pallid — not the rugged flush of a loyalist, but the pale, almost translucent skin of someone who has spent ten millennia in sensory extremity. Piercings, tattoos, and slaaneshi scarification are common. Paint the flesh deliberately — it's meant to be seen.

Excess Detail — Piercings & Modifications
Excess · Brush

Piercings — Gold or Silver Metallic Dots

EC models frequently have sculpted piercings — rings, studs, bars through flesh. At 28mm these are single raised dots or ring shapes. Apply Rich Gold 028 (for gold piercings) or VMC 77.720 Gunmetal Grey + Silver 025 edge (for silver piercings). A size 000 or rigger brush for this detail. Each piercing gets a single bright highlight dot on its upper face. If a piercing goes through flesh, apply a very thin wash of Flesh Wash 202 around its entry point — the flesh around a piercing is slightly inflamed in colour.

Excess · Brush — Optional

Slaanesh Tattoos — Pink Line Work

Some EC models have sculpted tattoos or slaanesh star markings on exposed flesh. If present, these are fine recessed lines. Apply Dark Magenta S10 undiluted with a size 000 brush into the recessed tattoo lines — the dark magenta reads as a crimson-purple tattoo in the skin. For any raised tattoo motifs, apply Pink 071 and highlight with Pale Pink 043. The tattoo colour should echo the armour's pink highlights, tying the model together visually.

Cracked marble tiles, gilded debris, pale rose-tinted dust. A base with pale stone tiles and gold fragments directly complements the armour's purple-and-gold palette and tells a story without a word. The pale stone contrasts the dark armour and the eye goes immediately to the model.

Airbrush Skills

The purple-to-pink gradient on Emperor's Children armour is the airbrush's strongest application here — it produces a smooth value progression from nearly-black shadow to vivid pink edge that would take many layers to achieve by brush alone. The key is disciplined pressure and nozzle control at each stage of the gradient build.

Troubleshooting

Purple Looks Brown / Muddy

Cause: Dark Purple 035 applied too opaquely over black, or the mid-tone (Purple 010) isn't covering enough to shift the colour away from the very dark shadow layer. Fix: Check your Purple 010 isn't over-thinned — at 1:2.5 it should have meaningful colour. Add a third pass of Purple 010 if needed. The brown reading usually means the dark shadow is dominating. More mid-tone passes, not less.

Pink Highlight Looks Garish / Disconnected

Cause: The Plum 070 step was skipped or under-applied, so the jump from Purple 010 (~42%) to Pink 071 (~80%) is too abrupt. Fix: The Plum 070 layer is the bridge. If it's not there, the value jump is too great. Apply thinned Plum 070 between the two layers and let it flash-dry before continuing.

Armour Edges Look White Not Pink

Cause: Pink 071 applied too thin and the Pale Pink 043 brush edge highlight is sitting on top of areas that never got the pink pre-highlight. Fix: The edge highlights will look correct once the whole model is complete — white edges appear stark in isolation. View the model from arm's length, not close up. If it still reads as pure white rather than pink, the Pale Pink 043 concentration is too high — thin it more or add a drop of Pink 071 to the mix.

Brush Skills

Emperor's Children reward precision — the purple armour's edge highlights are their visual signature, and they have to be crisp. The bone speaker horns need a smooth gradient from dark throat to bright bell. The flesh painting is deliberate and restrained. Each element requires a different brush technique.

Technique · Edge Highlighting

Purple Armour Edges — Consistency Over Speed

The pink edge highlight on purple armour is visually striking — it has to be clean. A single wobbly line reads as a mistake on EC armour more than on darker schemes where the edge highlight blends more forgivingly. Load a size 0 detail brush with Pale Pink 043 thinned 1:1. Wipe on a paper towel until the line you draw is thin, clean, and consistent. Rest your wrist on something stable. Place the brush tip at the start of an armour edge and pull in one motion — do not stop and restart, do not go back over a line while it's wet. If you make a mistake, wait until it's fully dry, then paint over with the flat armour colour (Purple 010) and redo when cured. Speed is the enemy here. Slow, deliberate, single strokes.

✦ TIP: Before highlighting the actual model, practise the exact stroke on a spare piece of card. Get three clean consistent lines in a row, then move to the model. This warm-up costs 30 seconds and prevents 30 minutes of rework.
Technique · Gradient Blending

Speaker Horn Throat-to-Bell Gradient

The bone speaker horns need a smooth shadow-to-light gradient from dark throat to bright bell rim. For a smooth transition by brush: apply the dark base (Dark Umber 019) and let it cure. Apply the mid-tone (Khaki 061) to the outer half of the horn, leaving the dark base at the throat. While the Khaki is still wet, use a small flat brush dampened with water (not loaded with paint) and drag from the Khaki boundary toward the dark base in short overlapping strokes — pulling the mid-tone into the shadow. This feathering technique avoids a hard edge at the transition. Repeat for the Bright Ivory 022 layer on the bell face. The goal is a gradient that reads as curved three-dimensional horn, not flat painted planes.

Technique · Targeted Washing

Flesh and Gold Recesses — Precision First

On Emperor's Children, the flesh and gold areas are visually precious — any wash that bleeds onto flat surfaces will show and need correction. Always use a fine liner brush for targeted washing, not a round brush. The liner brush's long thin bristles allow better capillary control. Touch the tip to the recess opening, allow the wash to flow in, then lift the brush before the wash escapes. Work in good light. If you have an Ott-Lite or similar daylight task lamp, have it aimed at the model — catching accidental wash overflow before it dries is essential.

Technique · OSL — Pink Sonic Glow

Painting Object Source Lighting

The sonic weapon's pink emitter creates a localised OSL effect on surrounding surfaces. OSL works by applying a very thin glaze of the light source colour to surfaces that would physically receive that light. The rule: the closer to the light source, the stronger the glaze; the further away, the more it fades to nothing. Apply Magenta 011 thinned to 1:5 on the armour immediately adjacent to the sonic emitter. Let it dry. If you can see the effect when you look carefully but it doesn't jump out, you've applied the right amount. OSL fails in two ways: too subtle (invisible) or too heavy (looks painted on). The test is to show the model to someone and ask if the weapon looks like it's glowing — if they say yes without prompting, you've nailed it.

Grimdark Weathering

Emperor's Children weathering is unlike any other Chaos Legion. The EC don't wade through mud. They don't let their armour rust. To do so would be an insult to Slaanesh — their armour is an extension of their vanity and devotion. Their corruption is sensory: ichor seeping from excess, the stain of pleasure worlds, dried blood from sensory experiments. Where a World Eater's armour is drenched in gore, an EC's armour has precise, deliberate marks of excess — each one intentional. Weather them accordingly: targeted, not broad. Two paths — Villainy Ink enamel or John Blanche Masterclass acrylic.

⚠ ENAMEL SAFETY — Read Before Starting Path A
Villainy Inks (VI-001 through VI-007) are oil/enamel-based. They require enamel thinner (white spirit) for cleanup — not water. Apply only over a fully cured acrylic varnish seal (MPAR-V01 or equivalent). Dry time: 4–12 hours. Keep white spirit away from your regular acrylic brush water. Never apply over unsealed acrylics — the thinner will lift paint. Work in a ventilated space.
Path A — Villainy Ink (Enamel)
Step 0 · Before Starting

Seal with MPAR-V01 Matte Varnish

Before any enamel work, apply a full coat of MPAR-V01 Pro Acryl Matte Varnish (spray can) to all completed acrylic layers. Let cure fully — minimum 2 hours, ideally overnight. The varnish acts as a barrier between the enamel and your painted surface. Without this step, the enamel thinner used in cleanup will lift your acrylic paint. This step is not optional.

Step A · Enamel

VI-001 Goon's Grime — Armour Joint Lines

Apply VI-001 Goon's Grime with a fine brush into all armour joint lines — the collar ring, pauldron hinges, torso-to-leg joins, knee articulation. The dark ochre-brown deposits in joint lines read as the accumulated residue of a being that has been in continuous excess for ten thousand years. Unlike the Thousand Sons' warp residue or the Salamanders' battlefield grime, the EC's joint fouling is dense and localised — the armour between joints should remain clean. Let capillary action do the work; do not apply broadly.

VI-004 Carrion Crimson — Ichor at Weapon Points & Flesh

This is the defining EC grimdark step. Do not make it look like a battlefield gore splatter — the EC's ichor is precise, intentional, concentrated. Let it pool slightly at the point of application rather than being wiped clean.

Step D · Seal & Finish

Final Seal — MPAR-V01 Then Targeted MPAM-003 Gloss

Once all enamel layers are fully cured (overnight), apply a second coat of MPAR-V01 Matte Varnish to lock in all enamel weathering. After curing, spot-apply MPAM-003 Gloss to: gems and lenses, the sonic weapon emitter face, the Carrion Crimson ichor points (which should glisten, not dry flat), and any exposed flesh that received the waxy specular treatment. The EC model should have a clear finish language: matte armour, glossy eyes and ichor, very subtle satin on the gold — excess expressed through finish variation.

Trouble Shooting

Enamel lifted the purple armour paint

Cause: Applied Villainy Ink to an unsealed surface.
Fix: Let enamel dry fully (4–12 hours). Touch up lifted area with Dark Purple 035, re-layer with Purple 010, and re-apply Pale Pink 043 edge highlight. Seal thoroughly before any further enamel work. The matte varnish seal is the only barrier between the enamel thinner and your acrylic layers.

Carrion Crimson spread too broadly — looks like blood splatter

Cause: Applied too liberally or not removed from flat armour surfaces quickly enough.
Fix (while still wet): Use a cotton swab barely dampened with enamel thinner to lift the excess off flat armour surfaces — leave the crimson only at the specific application points. EC weathering is deliberate and contained. If it looks like random gore, pull it back.
Fix (dried): Repaint the affected flat armour surface with Purple 010 and re-edge. The ichor at the intended points stays undisturbed.

Goon's Grime visible on armour plate faces, not just joint lines

Cause: Applied too broadly or didn't remove from surrounding plate surfaces.
Fix: Re-wet with enamel thinner on a cotton swab and drag across the plate face to lift. On purple armour the yellow-green of Goon's Grime is visible even in small amounts on flat surfaces. On EC this would be doubly offensive to the painter — the armour should be pristine between its deliberate weathering points.

Enamel thinner frosted or hazed the varnish

Cause: Acetone-based thinner used, thinner applied before varnish cured, or over-saturated swab.
Fix: Use white spirit or dedicated enamel thinner only — never acetone or IPA. Apply with a nearly-dry swab. If frosting occurred, a careful re-coat of MPAR-V01 matte varnish over the dried frosted area usually rescues it. Frosting is rarer on purple surfaces than on lighter colours — but still possible.
